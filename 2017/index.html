2017 personal Analytics dashboards

Inspired by The Personal Analytics of My Life by Stephen Wolfram, In 2014 I decided to make my own personal Analytics dashboards to track the changes in my daily routine. The hope is that I would find and enforce good trends while trying to spot and avoid bad trends.

This year my job title changed and the apps that I use in my day to day changed as well. More project management tools, left development tools. Netflix dropped from second place off the top 50 apps list. I made a effort to reduce my screen time this year.

- Protivitiy went way up this year to 71% of my tasks, while in 2016 it was 44%. Primiarly because of reducing screen time, netflix and social media. - listen to less music,. and more of the same bands over and over again then 2016 - Spent 4x times as much money this year on coffee then I did last year.

Rescue Time

These reports are created with RescueTime. The data comes from an app that is installed on my work and home PCs as well as my primary cell phone. The app only recoreds when the mouse and keyboard are active while using this app. This can distort the reportins for certain apps.

For example: In Visual Studio I spent most of my time typing and that time gets reported correctly. While watching a movie in Netflix, I don't move my mouse or type on my keyboard and their app dones not correctly report the time spent in these apps.
